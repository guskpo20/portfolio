## The End of an Era? ðŸ“‰

I recently came across a very interesting video titled **"Finally the METAVERSE is DEAD"**, and it makes a lot of sense given the latest news coming from Meta.

If you were betting on virtual reality meetings as the future of work, this news implies a significant shift.

### Goodbye Horizon Workrooms ðŸ‘‹

Meta has officially confirmed that **Horizon Workrooms will be discontinued as a standalone app on February 16, 2026**.

Launched in 2021, Workrooms was Meta's big bet to bring the office into the virtual world. The idea was ambitious: expressive avatars, virtual whiteboards, and the ability to see your computer screen inside VR. However, it seems adoption wasn't as high as expected.

### Why is Workrooms closing? ðŸ¤”

This move is part of a broader restructuring within Meta's **Reality Labs** division. According to reports, the company is:

1.  **Cutting costs**: There have been budget cuts and layoffs in the division.
2.  **Shifting focus**: Investment is moving from pure VR towards **wearables** and AI-powered smart glasses.
3.  **Simplifying**: Remote desktop functionality is now integrated into the Quest OS, making parts of the app redundant.

### What if I use Workrooms?

If you are an active user, you have until **February 16, 2026**, to download any important data. After that date, you will lose access.

Meta is not offering a direct first-party replacement for multi-user meetings but recommends third-party alternatives such as:
*   **Microsoft Teams Immersive**
*   **Zoom Workplace**
*   **Arthur**

### My take

It's interesting to see how the "Metaverse" narrative has shifted. From being "the inevitable future" to seeing its own flagship tools shut down. Perhaps the future isn't living *inside* a headset, but having a digital layer over the real world (Augmented Reality) with lighter devices.
